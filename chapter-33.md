# Chapter 33 — Tables

The first table to fall did not belong to a money changer.

This mattered.

It belonged to Naama.

It was small, old, local, and had one leg shorter than the others because Chuza had once repaired it after a child tried to climb it during a festival meal.

Mara said, "And?"

He smiled faintly.

"Not today."

Maryam nodded.

"Good."

They turned toward home.

Behind them, Jerusalem kept moving.

Ahead, the road held dust, work, rumor, and the next room.

For now, Daniel carried the fair weight.

Mara carried the feather.

Yosef carried the table wedge.

Maryam carried bread.

This felt like theology.

It was also lunch.

Good.

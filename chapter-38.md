# Chapter 38 — At Present

“At present” lasted nine hours.

No formal charge at present.

Unauthorized instruction monitored at present.

Financial accounts not declared unlawful at present.

Gatherings suspect at present.

Yeshua free at present.

At present was a door left open by someone standing beside it with a knife.

Mara said this while sharpening an actual knife, which made the metaphor worse and more accurate.

Pressure became small.

A seller refused Dinah unless she stopped asking rate questions.
Azriel intervened and lost customers.
A guard moved Natan along whenever he paused near the court.
Rinnah’s grinding group found oven time suddenly unavailable until Naama spoke with two witnesses and a loaf that smelled like accusation.
Yehudah’s account copies ran short after a supplier claimed lack of material; Shifra found another supplier and learned the first had been warned.

No arrest.

At present.

A cracked tally token arrived through Joazar.

Men were counting who entered Naama’s room.

Naama looked at it and said, “Then make their count wrong.”

Yehudah’s eyes lit.

“Confuse categories by telling the truth more fully.”

Exactly.

Not hiding.

Multiplying purpose.

People came for bread, repairs, routes, rates, rest, disputes, water, grief, labor records.
If watchers counted bodies and called all one thing, their account lied visibly.

They made a door rule:

Ask purpose before entry.

Feed if hunger.

Repair if object.

Listen if harm.

Redirect if curiosity.

Rest if fear.

Refuse if spectacle.

Record category, not vulnerable names.

Mara added: If someone says just watching, give them laundry.

The first man given laundry was Lemuel.

This improved morale.

He came pretending to look for Joazar, with no object and no question he would state.
Naama handed him a basket.

“I am not a servant,” he said.

“Then speak your purpose or leave.”

He folded three cloths badly before leaving.

The story traveled: They made Lemuel fold laundry.

Ruth’s correction traveled better: A watcher discovered cloth has corners.

Even Barak could not make that grand.

The larger request came through Mattithiah.

Not full council.
A test room.

Some wanted formal accusation.
Some warning.
Some public clarity.
Some Rome kept far away.
Some Rome closer.
Barak pressed.
Pedaiah measured.
Others feared crowds more than injustice.

Possible charges shifted like bad footing: disruption, misleading people, unauthorized teaching, contempt for temple order, possible claims of promise or kingship.

Kingship.

That word entered the room and brought Rome with it.

Maryam went pale.

Mara said, “No.”

No one corrected her.

Ruth had arrived by then, without permission, declaring stupidity had increased beyond local capacity.
She listened to Mattithiah and said, “Sleep.”

Everyone stared.

“Tired people offer themselves to traps because exhaustion feels like courage.”

Annoying.

Correct.

They set night structures, then slept badly.

Morning brought a crowd.

Small.
Mixed.
Worse.

Pilgrims, sellers, curious locals, women from Naama’s routes, one of Barak’s men, a guard at the edge, Lemuel looking aware of laundry.

Rumor said Yeshua would be expelled.
Or arrested.
Or speak.
Or bless the work.
Or condemn the temple.
Or leave.

Naama stood at the door.

“Purpose?”

Too many answered.

Maryam joined her.

“Food needs first.
Harm needs second.
Curiosity leaves.”

Some left.

Not many.

Yeshua stayed inside.

Correct.

Unbearable.

Yosef said, “Not every crowd is yours to answer.”

“I know.”

“Do you?”

“No.”

Ruth went out.

“If you came to see a man become important, you are late.
He is inside being told not to be foolish.
Go eat.”

Laughter moved.

A woman asked, “Will they arrest him?”

“Men with clean hands discuss many things,” Ruth said.

Not reassuring.

True.

A man shouted, “Is he against the temple?”

Naama answered, “He is against children going hungry because adults love procedure.”

The guard listened.

Careful.

Maryam said, “We are not against prayer.
We are against making prayer eat the poor.”

That traveled.

Everything traveled.

A man held up a broken sandal.

“Object!”

Mara said, “Damn.”

The crowd laughed.

The sandal belonged to a woman who had walked too far because rumor said Yeshua might leave and she wanted her sick child blessed before he went.
The child was not there.
The sandal was worn through; the strap had cut her foot.

Rumor had created travel.

Travel had created wound.

Good evidence.

Bad evidence.

Maryam sent water.
Daniel sent Erel and Joazar.
Mara went because Mara cleared paths like weather.

The woman arrived limping and ashamed.
The child had fever: not name-needed fever, but shade, water, cooling, watching, healer-needed fever.
Mattithiah found one.
Maryam stayed.
Daniel fetched cloth.
The sandal was repaired slowly because the foot mattered more than leather.

The at-present list gained:

Rumor creates travel.
Travel creates wounds.
Answer rumors by reducing travel, not increasing spectacle.

Mara added:

If people walk toward your importance, send water away from it.

Good.

The larger hearing was set for next morning.

Limited companions.

Isolation by procedure.

They replied by work category, not preference:

Mother and household witness.

Builder and object witness.

Mark and correction witness.

Account and purse witness.

Room and route witness.

Writing and condition witness.

Labor record witness.

Temple intermediary already present.

If a category is refused, questions in that category are incomplete.

Pedaiah hated it enough to allow most of it.

That night they made a garden-room inside.

A stone from the path.
Two sticks for exits.
Water.
No purse.
No tablet.
Ruth between Daniel and the doorway.
Erel present because exclusion would make him follow danger in his head.

Daniel knelt.

Not alone.

“I do not want to die.”

Still true.

“I do not want to be used.
I do not want silence to become cowardice.
I do not want speech to become performance.
I do not want fear to be king.
I do not want courage to be king either.”

Ruth muttered, “Good.”

“If there is a path through tomorrow, let it be carried by more than me.
If there is no path, do not let them make meaning before grief.”

Hands came, one by one.

Maryam.
Yosef.
Mara.
Erel.
Naama.
Rinnah.
Natan.
Yehudah after a pause.
Joazar.
Shifra.
Ruth, pressing too hard.

The garden answered with distributed weight.

Morning came without permission.

On one palm he wrote: answer second.

On the other: fear not alone.

Then they walked into at present’s end.

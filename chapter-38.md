# Chapter 38 — At Present

"At present" lasted nine hours.

This was longer than Mara expected and shorter than Natan hoped.

The phrase followed them back from the test room like a polite guard.
No formal charge would be made at present.
Unauthorized instruction would be monitored at present.
Financial accounts were not declared unlawful at present.
Public symbols remained prohibited at present.
Gatherings were suspect at present.
Daniel was free at present.

At present was a door left open by someone standing beside it with a knife.

Mara said this while sharpening an actual knife, which made the metaphor less helpful and more accurate.

Naama's courtyard had become too small for everyone's unease.
People came and went in deliberate patterns now.
No one moved alone.
Messages traveled in pairs.
Food routes continued but avoided certain corners.
Symbols were carried inside baskets, folded cloth, and children's memories rather than on standing boards.
The work had not stopped.
It had learned to duck.

Daniel hated ducking.

This was not a moral position.

It was a bodily one.

Ducking felt like fear becoming posture.
He knew better.
A person who refused to duck because he wanted to feel upright was still letting fear decide.
A low doorway did not become tyranny because one bent beneath it.

Still, he hated it.

Yosef noticed before anyone else.

"You are striking too hard."

Daniel looked at the peg in his hand.

It had split.

"Yes."

Yosef removed the hammer from his reach.

This was rude and wise.

Mara, from the doorway, said, "At present, your hammer privileges are suspended."

Daniel closed his eyes.

"Please do not make official phrases worse."

"They were born worse."

Maryam entered with food and the expression of someone who had been counting people rather than loaves.

"Eat."

"I ate."

"When?"

Daniel hesitated.

Mara pointed.

"Charge sustained."

Maryam set the bowl down.

"Food before strategy.
Food after test rooms.
Food while at present pretends to be time."

Daniel sat.

He ate.

This improved nothing except his ability to notice that nothing had improved.

Useful.

Yehudah arrived with Joazar and a small clay token cracked down the middle.
Not seal this time.
Token.
A tally marker used to count entries through a controlled storage door, according to Joazar.
It had been broken deliberately.

"By whom?" Maryam asked.

Joazar shook his head.

"Unknown."

Yehudah placed it beside the repaired purse.

"Men are counting who enters Naama's room."

Silence.

There it was.

Surveillance had become record.

Natan looked sick.

Naama looked angry in the way houses look before becoming fortresses.

Mara picked up the broken token.

"Good.
They made their own object."

Daniel almost smiled.

"What does it show?" Maryam asked.

Yehudah answered, "That watching has moved from curiosity to account."

Naama said, "Then we make their count wrong."

Yehudah looked at her with admiration.

"Yes."

Daniel did not like the admiration because he shared it.

Naama continued, "Not by hiding.
By multiplying purpose.
People come for bread, tools, disputes, repairs, water, sleep, rates, labor names.
If they count bodies and call all one thing, their account lies visibly."

Yehudah nodded.

"Confuse categories by telling the truth more fully."

Mara whispered, "She is terrifying."

Maryam said, "Good."

Natan said, "But if they count everyone, they can accuse everyone."

"At present," Mara said.

No one laughed.

Because true.

Daniel looked at the cracked token.

A broken count.

What is broken?

The assumption that entry equals allegiance.

What can be done?

Make entry inspectable.
Name reasons.
Not for watchers, but for protection.
Multiple routes.
Public mundane purposes.
Keep vulnerable people away when not needed.
Do not let fear empty the room.

He said, "No unnecessary attendance."

Mara looked at him sharply.

"Define necessary."

Good.

He hated that.

Maryam said, "Necessary includes being fed."

"Yes."

"Includes not being alone after threat."

"Yes."

"Includes correction."

"Yes."

"Does not include wanting to be seen near danger," Yosef said.

Erel, in the corner, looked down.

Good.

Mara said, "Does not include wanting to prove you are not afraid."

Daniel accepted that blow.

Yehudah said, "Does not include carrying information to feel important."

Joazar winced.

Good.

Natan said, "Does not include writing everything because silence frightens me."

Progress.

Naama said, "Does include old women who know where the water is stored, no matter what watchers think."

Excellent.

They made a door rule:

Ask purpose before entry.

Not to exclude.
To understand.

Feed if hunger.

Repair if object.

Listen if harm.

Redirect if curiosity.

Rest if fear.

Refuse if spectacle.

Record only category, not vulnerable names.

Mara added:

If someone says "just watching," give them laundry.

Naama approved.

This became policy.

---

The first man given laundry was Lemuel.

This improved morale.

He appeared at Naama's door near midday, pretending to look for Joazar.
He had no object, no food, no question he would state, and the unfortunate confidence of men who think observation is invisible if done with clean sleeves.

Naama asked, "Purpose?"

"I came to speak with Joazar."

Joazar, inside, looked up.

Naama asked Joazar, "Did you ask him?"

"No."

Lemuel flushed.

"I also wondered whether the Galilean was present."

Mara said, "Laundry."

Naama handed him a basket.

Lemuel stared.

"I beg your pardon."

"If you are waiting, fold."

"I am not a servant."

"Then speak your purpose or leave."

The courtyard watched.

This was risky.

It was also magnificent.

Lemuel looked at Daniel, who deliberately kept both hands on the peg he was not allowed to hammer.

Daniel said nothing.

Answer second.

Or not at all.

Lemuel looked at the basket.

Then, impossibly, took one cloth and folded it badly.

Mara whispered, "Jerusalem can still surprise."

Naama corrected his fold.

He endured three cloths before leaving.

By evening, the story traveled:

They made Lemuel fold laundry.

False version: The Galilean humiliated a city man.

Corrected version: Naama gave work to a watcher.

Better.

Even better version from Ruth by message: A watcher discovered cloth has corners.

This spread well.

Barak would not like it.

Good.

---

At present became a season of small pressures.

A seller refused to serve Dinah unless she stopped asking rate questions.
Azriel intervened publicly and lost two customers but gained three.
A guard moved Natan along whenever he paused near the court, whether or not he wrote.
Rinnah's grinding group found their oven time suddenly "unavailable" until Naama spoke to the oven owner with two witnesses and a loaf of bread that smelled like accusation.
Yehudah's account copies were delayed by lack of material after a supplier claimed shortage; Shifra found another supplier and learned the first had been warned.

No arrest.

No charge.

At present.

The phrase began appearing in jokes because humor was how frightened people tested whether fear had become king.

"At present, this bread is edible," Hanina wrote from home.

"At present, the lake has not drowned Shimon today," Dalia sent.

"At present, Erel has not become completely useless," Ruth added on a shared message, which Erel treasured despite himself.

Daniel wrote the jokes down and then crossed them out because writing jokes made them less funny.

Mara told him this was another skill he lacked.

He accepted the correction.

But pressure accumulated.

A basket with a warning symbol was slashed.

A child carrying a mark board was shoved.

A rumor spread that women attending Naama's room were neglecting households, which made Maryam so angry she cleaned three jars before speaking.

A second rumor said the accounts hid foreign money.

Yehudah opened the relevant books with inspectors present and found one questionable gift he had missed: coin routed through a cousin of Elior's old associate, likely clean in amount but dirty in intention.
He separated it, marked disputed, and sent it back with a note:

Gift rejected until its path can eat with those it affects.

Mara loved this more than was healthy.

Daniel asked Yehudah if he felt proud.

"Yes," Yehudah said.

"Too proud?"

"Probably."

"Structure?"

"Rinnah reads all rejected-gift notes before sending."

Good.

Small betrayals caught before roads.

For now.

---

The summons came again.

Not from Pedaiah.

From Mattithiah.

He arrived at dusk, unannounced, face gray with fatigue.

No object.

Maryam saw him and did not ask.

Good.

He sat only after Naama ordered him to.

"There will be a larger hearing," he said.

The room stilled.

"When?"

"Not yet named.
Soon.
Some want formal accusation.
Some want delay.
Some want him warned out of the city.
Some want him to speak plainly so they can sort danger.
Barak presses.
Others press against him.
The test room did not satisfy."

Mara said, "Because we answered too second."

Mattithiah almost smiled.

"Yes."

Yehudah asked, "Charges?"

"Not fixed.
Disruption, misleading people, unauthorized teaching, contempt for temple order, possible claims regarding promise or kingship depending on witnesses."

"Kingship?" Daniel said.

Mattithiah looked at him.

"Rome makes that word useful."

The room dropped.

There it was.

The word that could kill without theology.

King.

Messiah, teacher, prophet, healer—dangerous.
King—Roman danger.
The sacred machine could hand religious anxiety to political violence and wash its hands in procedure.

Maryam's face went pale.

Yosef's hands closed.

Mara said, "No."

No one corrected her.

Mattithiah continued, "Some do not want Rome involved.
Some fear any crowd.
Some think a warning and departure may avoid worse."

Shifra, who had arrived with Mattithiah and stood in shadow until now, said, "Leaving now will be named flight."

"Staying may be named defiance," Yehudah said.

"Both can be used," Natan said.

Naama said, "What cannot be used?"

No one answered.

Then Ruth, who had no business being in Jerusalem and therefore had arrived secretly with a neighbor's cart, said from the doorway, "Nothing.
So stop asking that."

Everyone turned.

Mara whispered, "Oh good."

Ruth entered with a bundle and no apology.

"I was told stupidity was increasing beyond local capacity."

Daniel stared.

"You came to Jerusalem?"

"At present."

Mara laughed.

Maryam went to her and embraced her.

Ruth endured it with dignity.

Then she looked at Mattithiah.

"Say again, but with less priest fog."

Mattithiah, to his credit, did.

A larger hearing.
Possible formal charge.
Political language possible.
Some want Daniel gone.
Some want him trapped into claim.
Some want him discredited.
Some want him dead? He did not know.
Not yet.
At present.

Ruth listened.

Then said, "Then sleep."

Everyone stared.

She looked annoyed.

"Not forever.
Tonight.
Tired people offer themselves to traps because exhaustion feels like courage."

Maryam closed her eyes.

"Yes."

Daniel hated that because he had been about to argue strategy.

Ruth pointed at him.

"Especially that one."

Mara nodded.

"Agreed."

Yosef said, "We set night structures, then sleep."

Good.

Night structures:

No one leaves alone.

Mattithiah sleeps in Naama's outer room if safe.

Ruth sleeps wherever she terrifies most efficiently.

Messages wait until morning unless urgent harm.

Daniel not to pray alone outdoors tonight.

Yehudah not to manage accounts tonight.

Natan not to write speculative charges tonight.

Mara not to threaten anyone with knives tonight unless necessary.

Mara objected to the vagueness of necessary.

Maryam defined it narrowly.

They slept badly.

But they slept.

---

Morning brought a crowd.

Not a large one.

Worse, a mixed one.

Pilgrims, sellers, curious locals, two students of Natan not authorized to be useful, one of Barak's men, several women from Naama's routes, a child with a coin mouth drawn on his arm, a guard at the edge, and Lemuel, who now seemed deeply aware of laundry.

They gathered because someone had said Daniel would be expelled.

Or arrested.

Or speak.

Or bless the work.

Or condemn the temple.

Or leave.

Rumors had decided to save time by becoming a crowd before anyone asked.

Naama stood at the door.

"Purpose?"

Too many answered at once.

Mara whispered, "Laundry insufficient."

Maryam stepped beside Naama.

"Food needs first.
Harm needs second.
Curiosity leaves."

Some left.

Not many.

Daniel stayed inside.

This was correct and unbearable.

Yosef stood near him.

"Not every crowd is yours to answer."

"I know."

"Do you?"

"No."

Good.

Ruth went out.

This startled everyone.

She looked at the crowd and said, "If you came to see a man become important, you are late.
He is inside being told not to be foolish.
Go eat."

A laugh moved through the crowd.

Good.

Then a woman called, "Will they arrest him?"

Ruth answered, "Men with clean hands discuss many things."

Not reassuring.

True.

Another asked, "Is he leaving?"

Ruth said, "Are you offering a safe road or gossip?"

The woman looked down.

A man shouted, "Is he against the temple?"

Naama answered this time.

"He is against children going hungry because adults love procedure."

The guard shifted.

Careful.

Maryam stepped forward.

"We are not against prayer.
We are against making prayer eat the poor."

Good.

Dangerous.

A murmur.

The guard listened.

Natan whispered from inside, "That will travel."

Mara said, "Everything travels.
Give it a limp."

Daniel stepped toward the doorway.

Yosef touched his arm.

"Why?"

Daniel stopped.

Why?

To help.

To answer.

To be seen.

To stop fear.

To make himself available so others were not.

To satisfy the crowd.

Bad mix.

He stepped back.

"Not yet."

Good.

Erel watched him with wide eyes.

Learning.

Hard lesson.

Outside, Ruth said, "Questions with objects after food.
Everything else returns later."

A man held up a broken sandal.

"Object!"

Mara said, "Damn."

The crowd laughed.

The sandal entered.

The crowd became a room by degrees.

Not all.
Enough.

Daniel did not appear as center.

The broken sandal belonged to the shouting man's sister, who had walked too far because rumor said Daniel would leave and she wanted her sick child blessed before he went.
The child was not there.
The woman had sent the sandal to prove the walk.
The sandal was worn through; the strap had cut the foot.

Maryam took the sandal.

Her face changed.

"Where is she?"

The man pointed.

"Outside the north lane."

"Bring her food and water," Maryam said.

People moved.

Daniel felt the old sickness: rumor causing travel; bodies paying for story.

He did step out then.

Not to speak to the crowd.

To send Erel and Joazar with water, and Mara because Mara could clear a path like weather.

Ruth watched him.

"Acceptable."

High praise.

When the woman arrived, limping and ashamed, the crowd had mostly dispersed into tasks.
The child had fever, not the kind that needed Daniel's name, the kind that needed shade, water, rest, cooling, watching, and perhaps a healer who knew more than they did.
Mattithiah helped find one.
Maryam stayed.
Daniel fetched cloth.
The sandal was repaired, but slowly, because the foot mattered more than leather.

The rumor became visible harm.

Good.

Bad.

Necessary.

They added to the at present list:

Rumor creates travel.
Travel creates wounds.
Answer rumors by reducing travel, not increasing spectacle.

Mara added:

If people walk toward your importance, send water away from it.

Good.

---

The larger hearing was set for the next day.

Pedaiah's notice arrived before noon.

Formal enough to frighten.

Informal enough to deny.

Daniel was requested to appear with limited companions to answer concerns before an expanded group of religious authorities and civic intermediaries.
Civic intermediaries meant men who understood Rome without admitting they were afraid of it.

Limited companions.

There it was.

Isolation by procedure.

Maryam read the notice.

"No."

Yehudah said, "They will not allow all."

"Then they must state who is excluded and why," Shifra said.

Natan said, "If we refuse limited companions, they can call it disorder."

Ruth said, "If they choose companions, they choose the body."

Good.

Yosef said, "Who must go?"

Maryam answered, "Daniel, but not alone.
Me."

"Yosef," Daniel said.

"Yes."

"Mara."

"Obviously," Mara said.

"Yehudah," Shifra said.

Everyone looked.

Yehudah too.

Shifra continued, "Financial organization is one concern.
If he is absent, they invent.
If present, risk.
But structure requires him."

Maryam nodded reluctantly.

"Naama," Ruth said.

"Room and routes," Naama agreed.

"Natan?" Daniel asked.

Natan looked torn.

Pedaiah would use him.
So would they.
Writing mattered.
Categories mattered.

Maryam said, "Yes, but answer only when your work is asked."

Natan nodded.

"Rinnah?" Yehudah asked.

"Labor record," Maryam said.
"Yes, if she chooses."

Rinnah chose.

Mattithiah would be inside regardless.

Joazar? Witness to Barak.
Shifra? Strategy but perhaps too visible.
Ruth? Not allowed by physics, perhaps, though no one dared say so.

In the end, the requested companions were named in reply by work category, not relationship:

Mother and household witness.

Builder and object witness.

Mark and correction witness.

Account and purse witness.

Room and route witness.

Writing and condition witness.

Labor record witness.

Temple intermediary already present.

They did not ask permission for Mara by name.

They asked for mark and correction witness.

Mara loved this.

The reply also said: if any work category is refused, questions in that category are incomplete and will be answered as such.

Pedaiah would hate it.

Good.

---

The night before the larger hearing, Daniel wanted the garden.

He did not go.

This was victory and loss.

Instead, the garden came inside.

Yosef placed a stone from the path in the center of the room.
Mara placed two small sticks to mark exits.
Maryam placed water.
Yehudah placed no purse.
Natan placed no tablet.
Ruth placed herself between Daniel and the doorway because subtlety had never been her craft.
Naama dimmed the lamp.
Erel sat near the wall, included because exclusion would make him follow danger in his head.
Rinnah came.
Joazar came.
Shifra came late and sat without speaking.

They made a garden-room.

Ridiculous.

Necessary.

Daniel knelt on the floor.

Not alone.

He said, "I do not want to die."

This was becoming less unspeakable and no less true.

Maryam breathed through pain.

Yosef closed his eyes.

Mara looked at the ground.

Yehudah did not look away.

Daniel continued, "I do not want to be used.
I do not want to save myself by letting others be used.
I do not want to answer as they demand.
I do not want silence to become cowardice.
I do not want speech to become performance.
I do not want fear to be king.
I do not want courage to be king either."

Ruth muttered, "Good."

He almost smiled.

"If there is a path through tomorrow, let it be carried by more than me.
If there is no path, do not let them make meaning before grief.
If I am wrong, correct me.
If I am afraid, do not shame me.
If I become shiny, insult me.
If I reach for a table, make me see who is under it."

Mara whispered, "Explicitly."

"Explicitly."

Maryam placed a hand on his shoulder.

One by one, others did too.

Not all at once.

That would have been too much.

Yosef.
Mara.
Erel, trembling.
Naama.
Rinnah.
Natan.
Yehudah after a pause long enough to be honest.
Joazar.
Shifra.
Ruth, last, who pressed too hard and said, "Sleep after."

Daniel laughed.

The room did.

Briefly.

Good.

The garden had not answered with a voice.

It answered with weight distributed.

For now, enough.

---

Morning came without permission.

The city looked almost ordinary, which Daniel considered one of its more manipulative qualities.

People bought bread.
Animals complained.
Children ran.
Men with power dressed.
Women who made everything possible had already been awake for hours.
The temple stood beautiful, terrible, and apparently unaware that Daniel's body had spent the night arguing with fear.

They ate.

Of course.

The group gathered.

Maryam adjusted Daniel's cloak.

Yosef checked the tool roll.

Mara checked her correction list.

Yehudah handed the purse keys to Naama before leaving, then showed empty hands.

Good.

Natan carried only the agreed tablets.

Rinnah carried names.

Naama carried routes.

Erel stayed with Ruth, which made him look as if he had been assigned to guard a storm.

Ruth told Daniel, "Answer second."

"I wrote it on my hand."

"Hands sweat."

"I wrote it again."

She inspected.

On one palm: answer second.

On the other: fear not alone.

Mara saw.

"Good."

Maryam saw and touched both hands.

Yosef saw and nodded.

Yehudah saw and looked away.

They walked.

At present walked with them.

This time, it felt shorter.

The hearing room waited.

So did Barak.

So did whatever came after at present ended.

Daniel carried no title.

He carried his marked hands.

For now, that would have to be enough.
